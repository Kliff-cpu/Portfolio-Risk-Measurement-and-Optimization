
# Portfolio Risk Measurement and Optimization

## Overview
This project analyses portfolio risk using multiple downside and tail-risk measures and compares how different optimization objectives affect portfolio outcomes.

The analysis covers market risk, drawdown risk and credit risk, giving a broader view of portfolio exposure under both normal and stressed conditions.

---

## Methodology

### 1. Risk Measurement
The project evaluates portfolio risk using:
- Daily Volatility
- Historical VaR
- Historical CVaR
- Normal VaR
- Normal CVaR

These measures provide a view of both day-to-day variability and tail risk under adverse scenarios.

### 2. Portfolio Optimization
Portfolio optimization was carried out under different risk objectives:
- CVaR
- EVaR
- CDaR

This allows comparison of how portfolio allocation changes depending on the definition of risk being minimized.

### 3. Credit Risk
The analysis also includes Total Credit VaR, incorporating credit exposure into the overall assessment of portfolio risk.

---

## Interpretation

The analysis shows that standard volatility measures alone are not sufficient to describe portfolio risk under extreme market conditions.

By comparing VaR, CVaR, EVaR, and CDaR, the project highlights how different risk measures capture different aspects of downside exposure. The inclusion of credit risk further broadens the assessment by showing that portfolio risk is not driven only by market movements, but also by potential credit-related losses.

This demonstrates the importance of selecting appropriate risk measures when making portfolio and risk-management decisions.
